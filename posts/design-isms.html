Design-isms

Mar 2, 2024  — 
Design

Design-ism: A word or phrase designers use that most people don’t understand.

Confusion #

Design-isms are getting out of hand, and it may require some re-thinking. Due to the lack of understanding surrounding them, these umbrella terms can mean different things to different people... adding to the confusion.

For the sake of clarity and to ensure our messages are accurately conveyed, we really should find simpler alternatives. Though this process may require more time and cognitive effort on the part of designers, it remains the most effective approach.

Instead of 'Personas', say: #

"We are in the process of developing a collection of fictional characters to embody various customer personas and illustrate their potential reactions in diverse scenarios."

Instead of 'A/B testing', say: #

"We are conducting a comparison test between two design variations to determine which one achieves superior performance."

Instead of 'Agile', say #

"Instead of constructing an entire app or website in one go, we're decomposing it into smaller components and accomplishing them in iterative cycles spanning 1-2 weeks each."

Instead of 'Mental Models', say: #

"Not all assumptions about how apps or websites function align with reality. By delving into these preconceptions, we can better align our design with people's actual understandings."

Instead of 'Information Architecture', say: #

"We're crafting a diagram aimed at organizing our entire content structure. Consider it as mapping out the pages and sections of a website, illustrating how users transition from one to another."

You get the gist #

Not everyone we communicate with will have a background in design or be familiar with design terminology. By using language that is accessible to all, we create a more inclusive and welcoming environment for collaboration and communication.
